- Journal for Dousilyn
**Entry 1 - Loneliness & Duty (6 months prior)** > _The silence here is different from the silence below. Down there, the stone whispers. Up here, there's only me and the hum of the portal machinery. Sindomi writes every week. I've started counting the letters like prayers. No replacement has been assigned in months. Others forget I exist, I think. But the post must be held. I swore an oath when I was thirty-five, young and certain of everything. That certainty feels like a foreign language now._ 
**Entry 2 - Discovery in the Jungle (3 months prior)** > _Found something in the jungle beyond the fortress perimeter today. A shrine—old, weathered, hidden beneath vine and time. No one knew it was there. The stone was smooth, worn by hands long gone. The carvings were strange, beautiful. I left an offering. Grain and mead, like my mother taught me. For the first time in years, I felt less alone. Something about that place listened in a way others never do. Maybe that's what faith is—finding something that listens even when the world forgets you._ 
**Entry 3 - The Disappearance (2 months prior)** > _Attlu is empty. The lizardfolk village to the north—gone. No bodies, no signs of violence. Just abandoned. Their boats are still there. Their fires still warm. I walked through once, slowly, respectfully. Called out in case anyone remained. Only echoes answered. I've left word with patrols, but I suspect no one will investigate properly. People disappear on this island. Perhaps this island takes what it needs._ 
**Entry 4 - Doubt & Decisions (1 month prior)** > _I have decommissioned the enchanting tables in the lower chamber. No one authorized me to do so. No one asked. But I am here, and they are not. I cannot allow unauthorized users to access these tools. Perhaps this is overreach. Perhaps this is the only thing I've ever done that matters. I've begun to wonder if Kaldroth still listens to us at all, or if we've been forgotten by gods as surely as gods forget mortals. The altar in these chambers still accepts offerings. I leave them anyway._ 
**Entry 5 - Final (2 days ago)** > _No replacement coming. Others' last message confirmed it—I am to hold this post until relieved or until I can no longer. That may be soon. A woman came through the portal today. Silver-blue hair like wind. She asked questions about what lay below. I told her nothing. She smiled like she already knew the answer. I think she has a crossbow, though I cannot be certain._ > _If you find this, know that I held the post. Know that I tried. If I am found dead, speak my name to Kaldroth—say that Dousilyn Ironseam kept the oath she swore at thirty-five. Say that she found the altar and left offerings. Say that she understood, at the end, that we are small and the world is vast, but we matter because we choose to matter. Say that the silence was less lonely when I remembered that._ > _The shrine—if anyone comes after me, tend to it. Someone should._
